In early 2011, [Hanselman](http://www.hanselman.com/blog/) published a [post](http://www.hanselman.com/blog/NewInterviewQuestionsForSeniorSoftwareEngineers.aspx) with a set of questions he considers useful when interviewing a Senior Software Engineer.

If you're looking to evaluate your senior developers, this list is quite interesting — but it's especially valuable for anyone who wants to become a senior, as it can serve both as a guide and as a self-assessment benchmark.

The original list (in English) can be found in his post. Below is my (non-literal) translation of the questions*:

- What is the most substantial thing you have done in your career to improve as a developer?
- Would you call yourself a craftsman (craftsperson) and what does that word mean to you?
- Implement a \<basic data structure\> using \<some language\> on \<paper\|whiteboard\|notepad\>
- What is SOLID?
- Why is the Single Responsibility Principle important?
- What is Inversion of Control? How does it relate to dependency injection?
- What is the difference between a 3-tier application and a 2-tier application?
- Why are interfaces important?
- What is the Repository pattern? And the Factory pattern? Why are patterns important?
- What are some examples of anti-patterns?
- Who are the Gang of Four? Why should you care?
- How do the MVP, MVC, and MVVM patterns relate? When are they appropriate?
- Explain the concept of Separation of Concerns and its pros and cons.
- Name three primary attributes of object-oriented design. Describe what they mean and why they are important.
- Describe a pattern that is NOT the Factory pattern. How and when would you use it?
- You have just been put in charge of a legacy code project with maintainability issues. What kinds of things would you do to improve the project toward a stable base?
- Show me a portfolio of all the applications you have worked on and tell me how you contributed to the design of each one.
- What are some alternatives to storing data that are different from relational databases? Why would you use them and what are the downsides?
- Explain the concept of convention over configuration and give an example of a convention over configuration you have seen in production.
- Explain the difference between stateless and stateful systems and the impact of state on parallelism.
- Discuss the difference between Mocks and Stubs/Fakes and where you might use them (the answers aren't as important here as the discussion that takes place).
- Discuss the concept of YAGNI and explain something you did recently that adhered to this principle.
- Explain what you understand by a sandbox, why you should use one, and identify examples of sandboxes in production.
- Concurrency. What is the difference between Locking and Lockless (Optimistic and Pessimistic) concurrency models?
- What kinds of problems can you have with a locking model? And with a lockless model?
- What trade-offs do you have for resource contention?
- How does a task-based model differ from a thread-based model?
- What is the difference between asynchrony and concurrency?
- Do you still write code? Do you love doing it?
- You have just been assigned to a project using a new technology. How would you get started?
- How does adding a Service Orientation change systems? When is it appropriate to use it?
- What do you do to stay current with the latest technologies and tools?
- What is the difference between "set" logic and "procedural" logic? When and why do you use each?
- What source control systems have you worked with?
- What is Continuous Integration? Have you used it and why is it important?
- Describe a software lifecycle that you have managed.
- How do you react when people criticize your code or documents?
- What blogs or podcasts do you follow? Do you publish on any blog or podcast?
- Tell me about some of your hobby projects that you have written in your free time.
- What is the last programming book you read?
- Describe, in as much detail as you think is relevant, as deeply as you can, what happens when I type "cnn.com" into a browser and press enter.
- Describe the structure and content of a design document, or a set of design documents, for a multi-tier web application.
- What is so great about \<current cool web technology\>?
- How can you stop your DBA from making off with a list of your users' passwords?
- What do you do when you get stuck on a problem you cannot solve?
- If your database is under a lot of stress, what are the first things you would consider to speed it up?
- What is SQL injection?
- What is the difference between unit testing and integration testing?
- Tell me about 3 times you have failed.
- What is Refactoring? Have you used it and is it important?
- Name three common refactoring techniques.
- You have two computers and you want to transfer data from one to the other. How would you do it?
- Left to your own devices, what would you create?
- Given Time, Cost, Customer Satisfaction, and Best Practices — how do you prioritize them in a project you are working on? Explain why.
- What is the difference between a web server, a web farm, and a web garden? How might your web application change between each?
- What value do daily builds, automated testing, and peer reviews add to a project? What are the downsides?
- Which OO design elements are most prone to being abused? How can you mitigate them?
- When do you know your code is ready to go to production?
- What is YAGNI? Is this list of questions an example?
- Describe some bad code you have read or inherited recently.

\* Some questions I felt were better left in English, as I could not find a translation that preserved exactly the same meaning.
