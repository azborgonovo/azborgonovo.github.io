We are almost passed the first half of the 2026 and I want to take a moment to archive relevant learning sources which have been useful to me and may be useful to others.

## The basics: AI literacy

With the EU AI Act around the corner, GoodHabitz (disclaimer: the company I currently work for) offered all it's employees access to [GoodLearn](https://www.goodhabitz.com/solutions/ai-literacy-skills). I have completed three courses: Introduction to GenAI, Data Literacy with AI and Visualize Your Data. What I like about GoodLearn is how accessible the content is through the mobile app. It feels like doing Duolingo on a very current and useful topic. If you are a company looking for ways to comply to the EU AI Act and, furthermore, enable it's workforce on AI reach out to the [GoodHabitz team](https://www.goodhabitz.com/product/goodlearn#request-form).

## Learning with Claude Code

In the last year I have switched from ChatGPT + GitHub Copilot to fully using Claude (and Claude Code).
During that transition I decided to take on the official [Anthropic courses](https://anthropic.skilljar.com/).
I strongly recommend anyone using Claude to take a few of these (or all) courses as they cover Claude's most important foundational concepts and features.
These are the ones I've taken so far:
- [Claude 101](https://anthropic.skilljar.com/claude-101)
- [Claude Code 101](https://anthropic.skilljar.com/claude-code-101)
- [Claude Code in Action](https://anthropic.skilljar.com/claude-code-in-action)
- [Introduction to agent skills](https://anthropic.skilljar.com/introduction-to-agent-skills)
- [Introduction to subagents](https://anthropic.skilljar.com/introduction-to-subagents)

## Matt Pocock (aihero.dev)

Matt Pocock was the creator of the [grill-me](https://github.com/mattpocock/skills/blob/main/skills/productivity/grill-me/SKILL.md) skill. If you haven't used `/grill-me` yet, try it now. Moreover, Matt is an educator who has moved fully into AI-assisted coding and offers both free and paid training material on conferences, YouTube and at [aihero.dev](https://www.aihero.dev/).

- [The AI Engineer Roadmap](https://www.aihero.dev/ai-engineer-roadmap)
- ["Software Fundamentals Matter More Than Ever" — Matt Pocock](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v4F1gFy-hqg)
- [Full Walkthrough: Workflow for AI Coding — Matt Pocock](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-QFHIoCo-Ko)

## Harness engineering: the term of 2026

Martin Fowler continues to excel both at writing splendly or inviting writers to his blog that are equally eloquent about tech.
I want to highlight three materials worth a read and study:
- [Humans and Agents in Software Engineering Loops](https://martinfowler.com/articles/exploring-gen-ai/humans-and-agents.html)
- [Harness engineering for coding agent users](https://martinfowler.com/articles/harness-engineering.html)
- [Podcast: What is harness engineering?](https://open.spotify.com/episode/5hokMt4blKtXOFQ31ftvkJ)

## Geoffrey Huntley

Geoffrey named and viralized [Ralph loops](https://ghuntley.com/loop/). His posts and videos provide inspiration for what AI-assisted coding can do today and what the future may bring.

In mid-2025 he shared a [six-month recap](https://ghuntley.com/six-month-recap/) which I still consider worth having a look for a general perspective of the impact and transformations brought by GenAI.
