I joined the AWS Summit Amsterdam for the first time in 2025. This year I got the chance to join again — and it was even better.

## First impressions

The expo floor felt noticeably less crowded than last year. Fewer stands, more space to walk around — and that made connecting with partners much more relaxed and enjoyable. I also noticed AWS running some diversity & inclusion and donation-related initiatives on the floor, which was a nice touch.

## Keynote

The keynote opened with a single provocative question: **"Why not?"** — and that energy carried through the full hour.

Karen Van De Woestyne (COO at KBC) was among the speakers, and the European angle was front and center: Benelux was highlighted as performing well among EU peers, and the expected AI impact on European GDP was discussed.

On the product side, the keynote spotlighted:
- **Amazon Quick** — framed as a tool to empower non-developers
- **AWS Transform** — for cloud modernisation
- **Kiro** — AWS's take on spec-driven development, which got a lot of stage time
- **AWS Lambda Durable Functions** — announced as a notable addition

The unifying thread: AI agents transforming what's possible, with AWS making it easier to build, deploy, and scale them. The three pillars of "the best place to build" — **Infrastructure, Data, Inference** — grounded the whole framing.

## AWS GameDay: Secret Agentic Unicorns

This was the highlight of the day. The GameDay, facilitated by Marcel van der Vliet (Senior Solutions Architect at AWS), dropped participants into a scenario: you are a new hire at Unicorn.Rentals, the world's largest mythical creature rental company. Your job? Complete a series of AWS challenges to earn points.

I teamed up with Sajad (from Good Habits) and two colleagues from Tata Consultancy. We pushed through the challenges step by step — and walked away with first place.

![1st Place trophy from AWS GameDay at AWS Summit Amsterdam 2026](/assets/images/aws-summit-amsterdam-2026/gameday-trophy.jpg)

## Workshop: Intelligently Automating Cloud Operations

The afternoon workshop, led by Nishant Gupta (Senior Delivery Consultant at AWS), brought things back to practical cloud ops. The session walked through connecting multiple AWS data sources via S3 buckets and using Amazon Quick as an interactive chat agent capable of querying across them intelligently.

Topics covered included AWS Health, Trusted Advisor, Amazon EventBridge, AWS Lambda, and GenAI/ML for cloud ops automation.

One thing I looked into afterwards: Amazon Quick uses **GraphRAG** under the hood. That's a promising technique worth keeping an eye on for anyone building AI-powered user experiences.

## The real takeaway

A colleague put it well — and I fully agree: **prioritize hands-on events**. The GameDay and the workshop gave me far more than the sessions where I was just watching. Deeper learning happens through doing.
